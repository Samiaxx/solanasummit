Creative Direction

The design language is culturally specific without becoming costume, premium without losing local texture, and clear enough for screen print production.

Cultural inspiration

  • Adire-inspired resist lines become circuit paths, linking Nigerian textile memory to internet capital markets.
  • Igbo uli curves, northern geometry, and woven band logic are abstracted into repeatable trims instead of literal costume references.
  • Akwa Ibom resort culture appears through the beach line: camp collars, terry textures, towels, totes, and relaxed summer silhouettes.

Solana connection

  • Summit lime and Solana mint act as the high-energy signal against deep event green and indigo.
  • Frame the typography around "Internet Capital Markets" as the central idea, not a secondary slogan.
  • Keep branding modular so every item can stand alone or stack into a collectible series.

Typography and symbolism

  • Condensed uppercase typography gives the collection a confident summit poster voice.
  • Small utility labels carry event data: Uyo, August 8, 2026, Superteam Nigeria, and Solana Summit Nigeria.
  • Brass accents represent value and craft; lime bands represent liquidity, movement, and onchain market energy.

Production notes

  • Use heavyweight 240gsm cotton tees, cotton twill overshirts, terry beach pieces, and recycled board packaging.
  • Keep each garment to 3-5 spot colors for reliable screen print and embroidery production.
  • Produce numbered limited editions for speakers, volunteers, sponsors, and collector packs.
